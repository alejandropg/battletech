# BattleTech Tabletop: Victory & End-of-Game Conditions

A standard game of BattleMech-vs-BattleMech combat continues, turn after turn, until one of the
end-of-game conditions below is met. At that point the match is over: no further phases are played.

---

## 1. Standard Victory

The game ends when **only one side still has surviving units** on the board — that is, all of every
other side's Mechs have been **destroyed** (see *Mech Destruction* in
[`armor-damage.md`](armor-damage.md)) or have **fled** off the map. The side with surviving units is
the **winner**.

A unit counts as "surviving" only if it is neither destroyed nor withdrawn from play.

---

## 2. Scenario Objectives

A scenario may define its own victory condition — capturing or holding an objective, surviving a fixed
number of turns, escorting a unit off a map edge, and so on. When a scenario objective is in force, it
can end the game (and decide the winner) independently of total elimination.

---

## 3. Draws (Mutual Destruction)

If the **last units on opposing sides are destroyed in the same resolution** — for example, two Mechs
that destroy each other in a simultaneous exchange — no side has surviving units. The game ends in a
**draw**.

---

> Destruction conditions that feed this check are defined in [`armor-damage.md`](armor-damage.md)
> (*Mech Destruction*) and [`pilot.md`](pilot.md) (pilot death).
