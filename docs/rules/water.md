# BattleTech Tabletop: Water Depth Effects

Water hexes have a **depth** that changes how a Mech standing in them moves, fights, dissipates heat,
and survives. Depth-1 partially submerges a Mech; depth-2 (or deeper) submerges it almost completely.

---

## 1. Depth-1 Water (Partially Submerged)

A Mech standing in depth-1 water is in the water up to its legs/hips:

- The Mech has **partial cover** (see [`line-of-sight.md`](line-of-sight.md) §3 for the +3 modifier and leg protection).
- Heat sinks submerged in the water dissipate extra heat (see [`heat.md`](heat.md) §1).

---

## 2. Depth-2+ Water (Fully Submerged)

A Mech in depth-2 or deeper water is almost entirely underwater:

- **Most weapons cannot fire** — only weapons rated for underwater use may attack.
- **Entering deep water** forces a Piloting Skill Roll (see [`pilot.md`](pilot.md)).
- If the cockpit floods — or the Mech is destroyed / falls underwater — the pilot risks **drowning**,
  which kills the MechWarrior over time (see [`pilot.md`](pilot.md)).

---

## 3. Movement

Water is **difficult terrain**: entering a water hex costs additional Movement Points, and deep water
both slows movement and forces the piloting rolls noted above.
